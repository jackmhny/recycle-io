2 - Always separate recyclables like paper, glass, and plastic from your regular trash.

4 - Avoid over-packaged goods and support companies with sustainable packaging.

6 - Repair broken items instead of throwing them away whenever possible.
7 - Donate clothes, furniture, and electronics instead of sending them to landfills.
8 - Learn your community’s recycling rules to avoid contaminating recyclable materials.
9 - Choose digital receipts and documents to cut down on paper waste.
10 - Buy in bulk to minimize packaging waste and reduce frequent trips.
11 - Properly dispose of hazardous materials like batteries, paint, and electronics.
12 - Teach others about the importance of reducing, reusing, and recycling to protect our planet.
13 - Use leftover food creatively to reduce food waste at home.
14 - Turn old jars, bottles, or containers into useful storage instead of throwing them away.
15 - Choose eco-friendly cleaning products that don’t harm the environment.
16 - Recycle used paper into crafts or note paper instead of buying new ones.
17 - Organize or join a community clean-up to help reduce litter in your area.

19 - Support recycling programs and encourage others to take part.
20 - Think before you throw — many items can be repaired, reused, or recycled.
21 - Teach friends and family how waste affects wildlife and natural habitats.
